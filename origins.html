WHERE IT ALL BEGAN.

1970 - The Bronx in flames

In the 1970s, the Bronx was burning - poverty, unemployment, and violence dominated the streets. Amid chaos, young people created a new form of expression: rhythm, color, and rebellion.

1973 - The first party

At 1520 Sedgwick Avenue, DJ Kool Herc extended breakbeats on twin turntables, sparking the birth of Hip Hop. Music, dance, art, and word became one movement.

1979 - Rap hits the radio

“Rapper's Delight” by The Sugarhill Gang brought rap to the airwaves and turned a local sound into a worldwide phenomenon.

1984 - Graffiti takes the subway

Artists like Dondi, Futura 2000, and Lady Pink transformed the New York subway into a moving gallery — and graffiti into art.

1990s - From the block to the world

Hip Hop became a global voice of identity and empowerment, shaping music, fashion, and culture across continents.

2000s - The legacy lives on

Today, Hip Hop continues to evolve as a global movement — from the Bronx to every corner of the world.
